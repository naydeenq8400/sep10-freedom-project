# Content
Since my last blog, I have moved on from from only researching justice system technology to also working  with my tool, A-Frame, which allows me to create 3D scenes. Instead of just learning what Aframe is, I started building objects myself to understand how positioning and shapes work. One of the main things I focused on was creating simple objects using basic shapes like boxes and cones. For example, I tried to build a house by stacking a cone on top of a box. This helped me understand how objects are placed using coordinates. In A-Frame, position is based on three values (x, y, z). The X controls left and right, the Y controls up and down, and the Z controls forward and backward.

# Challenges
1. At first, I struggled with positioning, especially getting the cone to sit correctly on top of the box. Even when I rotated the cone so the flat side faced downward, it wasn’t in the right place. After reviewing how coordinates work, I realized I needed to fix each value one at a time. Eventually, I got the correct positions: the box was at (-1, 0.5, -3) and the cone was at (-2, 2, -4). This helped me better understand how 3D space works and how the numbers in positioning need to be very exact, or it will not always turn out exactly like I visualized it.
